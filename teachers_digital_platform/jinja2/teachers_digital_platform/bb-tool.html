The journey to financial well-being

As children grow, their ability to manage money and understand financial concepts grows with them. Financial capability stems from three interconnected building blocks of youth development. Understand how the building blocks work together to help young people achieve financial capability and learn strategies for teaching them in your classroom.

This tour highlights four steps on the journey to adult financial well-being. These parts provide an innovate approach to financial education to help educators help their students.

Adult financial well-being

A key measure of success for financial literacy education is the degree to which it improves financial well-being.

But for many adults, financial well-being is more than the ability to do their taxes, track their spending, or balance a checking account. It’s the ability to make informed choices. This ability often relies on attitudes, skills, and habits developed during youth.

That’s where you come in.
